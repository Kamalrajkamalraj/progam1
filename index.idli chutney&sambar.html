    Idli Recipe        
    Ingredients:                        

    .1 cup urad dal (split black gram)  
    .2 cups idli rice (parboiled rice)  
    .¬Ω teaspoon fenugreek seeds         
    .Salt to taste                      
    .Water as required                  
    .Oil (for greasing)                 
     
    Idli, Sambar, and Coconut Chutney are classic South Indian dishes, 
and they complement each other perfectly. Here‚Äôs a detailed step-by-step guide for each:
           
Method
    1.Soak the Ingredients:

        Wash and soak urad dal and fenugreek seeds in water for 4-5 hours.
        Wash and soak idli rice separately for 4-5 hours.
    2.Grind the Batter:

        Grind the urad dal and fenugreek seeds with water until smooth and fluffy.
        Grind the soaked rice separately into a slightly coarse paste using water.
        Mix both batters in a large bowl, add salt, and leave it to ferment 
        overnight (8-12 hours) in a warm place.
    3.Steam the Idlis:

        Once the batter is fermented, stir it gently. Grease idli molds with oil and 
        pour the batter into the molds.
        Steam in an idli steamer or pressure cooker (without the whistle) for about 
        10-12 minutes.
        Check by inserting a toothpick or knife; if it comes out clean, the idlis are ready. 
        Allow them to cool slightly and demold them.

            
    Sambar Recipe        
    Ingredients:                        
    .1 cup toor dal (pigeon peas)       
    .1 small onion (chopped)            
    .1 tomato (chopped)                 
    .1-2 carrots (chopped)              
    .1 drumstick (chopped)              
    .¬Ω teaspoon turmeric powder         
    .1 tablespoon tamarind pulp         
    .1-2 green chilies (slit)           
    .1 teaspoon sambar powder (optional)
    .Salt to taste                       
    .For Tempering:                      

    .1 tablespoon oil or ghee           
    .1 teaspoon mustard seeds           
    .¬Ω teaspoon cumin seeds             
    .1 dried red chili                  
    .1 sprig curry leaves               
    .Pinch of asafoetida (hing)         
                 

    
Method
  Cook Dal:

    Wash toor dal and pressure cook it with turmeric powder, enough water, and a little salt for about 3-4 
    whistles until soft.
    Mash the dal and set aside.
  Prepare Vegetables:

    In a pan, add some oil and saut√© onions until soft. Add tomatoes, carrots, drumstick, and green chilies.
    Cook until the vegetables are soft.
    Add sambar powder and tamarind pulp. Let the mixture simmer.
  Combine Dal and Vegetables:

    Add the mashed dal to the vegetable mixture. Add enough water to reach your desired consistency.
    Let the sambar boil for 10-15 minutes.
  Tempering:

    In a small pan, heat oil or ghee, add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, red chili, curry leaves, and asafoetida.
    Pour the tempering into the sambar and mix well.


            
    Coconut Chutney Recipe        
Ingredients:                                            

    1 cup grated fresh coconut                          
    2 tablespoons roasted chana dal (split roasted gram)
    1 small green chili (adjust to taste)               
    1 small piece of ginger                             
    Salt to taste                                       
    Water (as required)                                 
For Tempering:                                          
            
    1 teaspoon oil                                      
    ¬Ω teaspoon mustard seeds                            
    ¬Ω teaspoon urad dal                                 
    1 dried red chili                                   
    Few curry leaves                                    

    
Method
  Grind Chutney:

    In a blender, add grated coconut, roasted chana dal, green chili, ginger, and salt. 
    Blend with water to a smooth consistency.
    Transfer to a bowl.
   Tempering:
    
    Heat oil in a small pan, add mustard seeds, urad dal, red chili, and curry leaves.
    Once mustard seeds splutter, pour this tempering over the chutney.
